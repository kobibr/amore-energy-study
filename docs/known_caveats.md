# Known Caveats — AmorE Energy Study

## CRITICAL — measurement calibration

### C1. PPK2 NOT calibrated against a known resistor

**Status:** The May 20, 2026 calibration attempt failed (expected 100 mA
through a 33Ω resistor, measured 45 mA; -55% deviation). This means
**absolute PPK2 current readings cannot be trusted.**

**Implication:** All energy/current numbers in this work are
**relative** (one configuration vs. another) and **not absolute**.

### Permitted claims (relative)

These are safe because any constant calibration factor cancels:
- AmorE/Direct ratio per curve (e.g. "AmorE @ N=30 is 3.91× per round
  vs. direct pairing under baseline firmware")
- Amortization slope (e.g. "energy/round decreases by 60% from N=1
  to N=30 for BN254 Mode A")
- Cross-curve ratio at same N (e.g. "BLS/BN254 ≈ 1.78× at N=30")
- Crossover behavior (e.g. "no crossover in [1,100] under baseline,
  crossover at N*=2 with Stop-mode for BN254")
- Phase-share fractions (e.g. "ServerWait dominates ~60% of batch energy")

### Forbidden claims (absolute)

These are unsafe until calibration:
- "AmorE consumes X mJ per round" (absolute mJ)
- "Total energy for N=30 batch is Y J" (absolute J)
- Direct comparison of MCU energy with datasheet radio energy
  (e.g. fig BLE/LoRa crossover with absolute mJ on both axes)

### Required to upgrade to absolute

1. Acquire 33Ω (or similar known-value, ±1% tolerance) resistor
2. Run `python3 -m analysis.calibrate --tolerance-pct 2.0` with
   the resistor between PPK2 VOUT and PPK2 GND (no STM32 connected)
3. Verify the verdict reads PASS with deviation < 2%
4. If FAIL, investigate (PPK2 device defective, voltage source mode
   issue, or measurement code bug)
5. Until PASS achieved, all absolute claims must be qualified
   in the paper as "uncalibrated; ratio analysis only"

### C2. calibrate.py bug — observed deviation likely artifactual

The 45 mA measured during the May 20 calibration may not reflect
real PPK2 inaccuracy. Suspect causes:

- `time.sleep(0.5)` before `start_measuring()` may not be enough
  for PPK2 to stabilize source-mode output
- Samples collected during `toggle_DUT_power("OFF")` cleanup
  may have been included in the stats
- stdev=64 mA on mean=45 mA (CV=141%) is consistent with mixed
  "on" + "off" samples, not Gaussian noise around a true value

**Action:** Investigate `analysis/calibrate.py` before re-running.

### C3. Calibration is NOT in the regular pipeline

`run_benchmark.sh` and `sanity_check.sh` do not invoke `calibrate.py`.
No automated check enforces that calibration has been run recently
or that the most recent calibration PASSed.

**Recommended:** Add a `sanity_check.sh` gate that refuses to run if
`measurement/calibration-logs/` contains no PASS entry from the last
7 days.

## DATA-LEVEL CAVEATS

### D1. Most current measurements are synthetic (mock PPK2)

`measurement/traces/*` cells were generated by the mock PPK2 backend
(`measurement/backends.py`) during development. They are physically
plausible (gauss-noisy current with phase tags) but are NOT real
PPK2 captures. The watermark on every figure says "SYNTHETIC DATA"
to make this explicit.

**Required for paper:** Re-collect all cells with real PPK2 hardware
once calibration passes. Current real-PPK2 runs:
- HONEST_ROUNDS=1 (2026-05-22 14:00, accepted=1)
- HONEST_ROUNDS=61 in progress (2026-05-22 21:46)

### D2. Voltage sensitivity data IS real

`measurement/voltage-sensitivity/voltage_*.csv` files from May 20
ARE real PPK2 captures (not mock). They support the relative
voltage-sensitivity claim but are subject to the same calibration
caveat.

### D3. WITH_STOP scenario is modeled, not measured

The `WITH_STOP` data in fig4_crossover is generated by the
`BatchModel` projection (assuming ServerWait → 0.5 µA), not by
firmware actually entering Stop mode. The paper must state this
explicitly. Reads as:

> "We model the effect of entering Stop mode during ServerWait
>  by substituting the 0.5 µA datasheet figure for our measured
>  ~55 mA busy-wait current. Implementing actual Stop-mode firmware
>  and re-measuring is future work."

## STATISTICAL CAVEATS

### S1. Variance is from replicas of the SAME chip

Each cell is 3 replicas on a single STM32F407 board. Variance reflects
**run-to-run** consistency, not chip-to-chip variability. A different
Discovery board may give systematically different absolute readings
(possibly within calibration noise) but should give the same
**ratios** to first order.

### S2. CV thresholds are diagnostic, not statistical

The variance_study CV=0.22% claim refers to byte-2 phase repeatability
within a single sweep. It is NOT a confidence interval for absolute
energy in any inferential sense. Treat as a quality control metric.

### S3. No physical model of temperature effects

The MCU heats during the high-power phases (compute, OTS). Later
rounds in a long sweep may show different energy because of
temperature-dependent Vcore regulator efficiency. We have not
characterized this; see future_work.md.

## RUNTIME ANOMALIES (observed but not understood)

### R1. STM32 silent after ~12 rounds in HONEST_ROUNDS=61 sweep

**Observed:** 2026-05-22 21:46 — sweep ran for 12 honest rounds at
~87s/round, then Round 13 STM32 did not send CMD_SETUP within the
server's 60s timeout. The sweep then aborted at the GDB telemetry
stage with "Remote connection closed".

**Suspect causes (untested):**
- Thermal: ~17 minutes of continuous pairing computation may warm
  the Vcore regulator past its quiescent point, drifting the clock
  or upsetting Flash wait-state timing.
- Cycle counter overflow: DWT_CYCCNT at 168 MHz wraps every ~25 s.
  If the firmware accumulates cycle counts without correctly handling
  overflow, the per-round metric may corrupt after many rounds.
- Server-side memory: py_ecc may retain state across rounds; not
  yet profiled.
- Random UART glitch (single-shot, not periodic).

**Workaround in this report:** HONEST_ROUNDS=12 is the longest
verified clean run as of this writing. Mean compute time appears
stable at ~87s with <1% drift across 12 rounds, so the data is
usable. Reproducing N=10/N=50 amort/round numbers requires only
12+ rounds successfully completed at each N, so this caveat does
NOT block the audit_table.

**Observed:** 2026-05-22 22:15 sweep — the N=10 batch reported
verify_ok=9 verify_fail=1 with the failure in round 9. The

Round 9's server compute time was 87,329 ms — squarely in the
normal range (87,076 to 88,045 ms across the other 9 honest
rounds). Round 11 (the malicious round) took 89,687 ms,
noticeably longer, and was correctly rejected.

**Ring-log:**
```
phase=0x13 batch=1 round=9 cycles=2668728286 extra=0x00000000  ← verify start
phase=0x13 batch=1 round=9 cycles=2739028506 extra=0x00000010  ← FAIL
phase=0x14 batch=1 round=9 cycles=2739028524 extra=0x00000000  ← status
```
Verify took 418 ms (cycles delta 70,300,220 @ 168MHz) which is
normal verify-time range (N=1 batch verify was 412 ms).

**Counter in firmware (src/amore.c:378):**
```c
res->rounds_verify_fail[bi]++;
```
This counter is incremented when the client-side verify check
returns false. The implementation of that check is what needs
investigation.

**Suspect causes (untested):**
- BLS12-381 arithmetic edge case: an intermediate value lands
  on a special point (e.g.\ identity in G_T) where the equality
  comparison is computed differently
- py_ecc server-side returned a value that is **mathematically
  valid** but differs from what AmorE's verification expects

- Random blinding factor used in verify generates a non-trivial
  algebraic cancellation that the verify did not anticipate
- A bit-flip somewhere outside UART (CRC=0 rules out transit)

1. Re-run HONEST_ROUNDS=10 two more times — if 0-1 fail per 10,

3. Compare py_ecc canonical-form choices with our verify

**Impact on paper claims:**
- ✓ "Verify catches malicious rounds" — still supported (round
  11 was correctly REJECTED)
- ⚠ "Verify has zero false positives on honest rounds" — NOT
  supported by this trial. Paper must acknowledge that we
  observed 1 FP in 10 honest rounds (single trial; needs more
  data to bound the rate)

### R1 (UPDATE 2026-05-23): server.py timeout=120s is the abort mechanism

The server's `recv_packet(port, timeout_s=120.0)` at server.py line ~256
is what produces the "No more packets for 120s — assuming benchmark
complete" message. So when STM32 went silent at Round 13 of the n61
sweep, this 120s timer is what called it.

This means the abort is correctly diagnosed by the server side; the
question for R1 investigation is **why STM32 stopped sending** —
not what the server did about it. The server's behavior is reasonable
(abort + report partial results) given the silent client.

Suspect causes for STM32 silence (unchanged from original R1):
- DWT_CYCCNT overflow (32-bit @ 168 MHz wraps every 25.6 s)
- Thermal: vcore regulator under sustained load
- UART buffer overrun on STM32 side
- Memory issue on RPi server (py_ecc allocation growth?)


## PPK2 Not Calibrated (2026-05-23)

### Status
`modifiers['Calibrated'] == '0'` — factory calibration was not loaded
into PPK2 EEPROM (calibration kit unavailable on this bench).

### Effect
- Absolute current readings are NOT trustable. With nothing connected,
  PPK2 reports ~55 mA instead of 0.
- Each ADC range has its own offset (modifiers['O'] = {73..161 µA}),
  so the bias is **piecewise-linear across ranges**, not globally
  linear.

### What IS safe to report
- **Ratios within the same current range** (e.g. AmorE-active vs
  Direct-active, both ~50–90 mA). Linear bias cancels.
- **Relative comparisons within one session.**

### What is NOT safe to report
- Absolute energy figures (µJ, mJ) — DO NOT cite.
- Comparisons spanning ranges (Stop @ 0.5 µA vs Active @ 85 mA).
- Any sub-1% claim.

### Required paper disclosure
"PPK2 used in factory-default (uncalibrated) state; reported values are
relative ratios within the active-phase current range."

### Resolution path
Nordic PPK2 calibration kit + nRF Connect Power Profiler calibration
utility → re-validate via `analysis/calibrate.py` with 33 Ω reference.
