# Known Caveats — AmorE Energy Study

## CRITICAL — measurement calibration

### C1. PPK2 NOT calibrated against a known resistor

**Status:** The May 20, 2026 calibration attempt failed (expected 100 mA
through a 33Ω resistor, measured 45 mA; -55% deviation). This means
**absolute PPK2 current readings cannot be trusted.**

**Implication:** All energy/current numbers in this work are
**relative** (one configuration vs. another) and **not absolute**.

### Permitted claims (relative)

These are safe because any constant calibration factor cancels:
- AmorE/Direct ratio per curve (e.g. "AmorE @ N=30 is 3.91× per round
  vs. direct pairing under baseline firmware")
- Amortization slope (e.g. "energy/round decreases by 60% from N=1
  to N=30 for BN254 Mode A")
- Cross-curve ratio at same N (e.g. "BLS/BN254 ≈ 1.78× at N=30")
- Crossover behavior (e.g. "no crossover in [1,100] under baseline,
  crossover at N*=2 with Stop-mode for BN254")
- Phase-share fractions (e.g. "ServerWait dominates ~60% of batch energy")

### Forbidden claims (absolute)

These are unsafe until calibration:
- "AmorE consumes X mJ per round" (absolute mJ)
- "Total energy for N=30 batch is Y J" (absolute J)
- Direct comparison of MCU energy with datasheet radio energy
  (e.g. fig BLE/LoRa crossover with absolute mJ on both axes)

### Required to upgrade to absolute

1. Acquire 33Ω (or similar known-value, ±1% tolerance) resistor
2. Run `python3 -m analysis.calibrate --tolerance-pct 2.0` with
   the resistor between PPK2 VOUT and PPK2 GND (no STM32 connected)
3. Verify the verdict reads PASS with deviation < 2%
4. If FAIL, investigate (PPK2 device defective, voltage source mode
   issue, or measurement code bug)
5. Until PASS achieved, all absolute claims must be qualified
   in the paper as "uncalibrated; ratio analysis only"

### C2. calibrate.py bug — observed deviation likely artifactual

The 45 mA measured during the May 20 calibration may not reflect
real PPK2 inaccuracy. Suspect causes:

- `time.sleep(0.5)` before `start_measuring()` may not be enough
  for PPK2 to stabilize source-mode output
- Samples collected during `toggle_DUT_power("OFF")` cleanup
  may have been included in the stats
- stdev=64 mA on mean=45 mA (CV=141%) is consistent with mixed
  "on" + "off" samples, not Gaussian noise around a true value

**Action:** Investigate `analysis/calibrate.py` before re-running.

### C3. Calibration is NOT in the regular pipeline

`run_benchmark.sh` and `sanity_check.sh` do not invoke `calibrate.py`.
No automated check enforces that calibration has been run recently
or that the most recent calibration PASSed.

**Recommended:** Add a `sanity_check.sh` gate that refuses to run if
`measurement/calibration-logs/` contains no PASS entry from the last
7 days.

## DATA-LEVEL CAVEATS

### D1. Most current measurements are synthetic (mock PPK2)

`measurement/traces/*` cells were generated by the mock PPK2 backend
(`measurement/backends.py`) during development. They are physically
plausible (gauss-noisy current with phase tags) but are NOT real
PPK2 captures. The watermark on every figure says "SYNTHETIC DATA"
to make this explicit.

**Required for paper:** Re-collect all cells with real PPK2 hardware
once calibration passes. Current real-PPK2 runs:
- HONEST_ROUNDS=1 (2026-05-22 14:00, accepted=1)
- HONEST_ROUNDS=61 in progress (2026-05-22 21:46)

### D2. Voltage sensitivity data IS real

`measurement/voltage-sensitivity/voltage_*.csv` files from May 20
ARE real PPK2 captures (not mock). They support the relative
voltage-sensitivity claim but are subject to the same calibration
caveat.

### D3. WITH_STOP scenario is modeled, not measured

The `WITH_STOP` data in fig4_crossover is generated by the
`BatchModel` projection (assuming ServerWait → 0.5 µA), not by
firmware actually entering Stop mode. The paper must state this
explicitly. Reads as:

> "We model the effect of entering Stop mode during ServerWait
>  by substituting the 0.5 µA datasheet figure for our measured
>  ~55 mA busy-wait current. Implementing actual Stop-mode firmware
>  and re-measuring is future work."

## STATISTICAL CAVEATS

### S1. Variance is from replicas of the SAME chip

Each cell is 3 replicas on a single STM32F407 board. Variance reflects
**run-to-run** consistency, not chip-to-chip variability. A different
Discovery board may give systematically different absolute readings
(possibly within calibration noise) but should give the same
**ratios** to first order.

### S2. CV thresholds are diagnostic, not statistical

The variance_study CV=0.22% claim refers to byte-2 phase repeatability
within a single sweep. It is NOT a confidence interval for absolute
energy in any inferential sense. Treat as a quality control metric.

### S3. No physical model of temperature effects

The MCU heats during the high-power phases (compute, OTS). Later
rounds in a long sweep may show different energy because of
temperature-dependent Vcore regulator efficiency. We have not
characterized this; see future_work.md.

## FORMATTING / DOCUMENTATION

### F1. Watermark must remain visible in published figures

`analysis/_figure_watermark.py` adds a red box with caveat text
to each figure. This MUST stay visible in any published version
until calibration is achieved.

### F2. audit_table.csv `status` column is authoritative

When citing a number in main.tex, check the corresponding row's
status:
- `measured`: real PPK2 capture, relative-only (until calibration)
- `computed`: derived from datasheets or models
- `pending`: not yet measured (do NOT cite)
